Thai Noodle Soup

Serves 4

Total Time: 20 min Active Time: 5 min

PREP:

  • 3-4 inches fresh ginger root
  • 1 bunch green onions
  • 2 qt vegetable broth
  • 12 oz dry noodles (i.e. vermicelli)
  • 2-4 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 6 tbsp sweet red chili sauce
  • 2-3 tsp miso paste
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil
  • ground ginger (optional)
  • red pepper flakes (optional)
  • mince
  • slice
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  

DIRECTIONS:

  1. In a large pot, combine broth and minced ginger (1-2 tbsp).
  2. Cover, bring to boil.
  3. Add noodles and cook as directed (see package).
  4. Turn off heat and stir in soy sauce, chili sauce, miso, and basil.
  5. Taste, adding more soy sauce or miso as desired, or ground ginger for a stronger flavor.
  6. Garnish with green onions and red pepper flakes, if using.

Per serving: 400 calories, .3g fat, 91.9g carbs, 4.0g fiber, 12.1g sugars, 6.3g protein,

Slimming: Add 15-oz tofu (cubed) or chickpeas, 1 cup peas and 2-3 cups cooked rice instead of using noodles (optional).
Make Ahead: Yes.

Samosa Chowder

Serves 4

Total Time: 25 min Active Time: 10 min

PREP:

  • 1 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 qt vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp mild curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 bag (12-oz) cauliflower florets
  • 1 can (14-oz) lite coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cup peas
  • 4 pitas or crackers (170 calories per serving)
  • dice
  • mince
  • dice

  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Line a large pot with ¼-cup broth.
  2. Sauté onions and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add spices, stirring to coat.
  4. Cook for a minute, then add remaining broth, potatoes and cauliflower.
  5. Cover and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low and simmer until potatoes are very tender, 15-20 minutes.
  7. Stir in coconut milk.
  8. Transfer HALF to a blender, purée until creamy and return to pot, OR use an immersion blender.
  9. Stir in peas.
  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Serve warm with pitas or crackers.

Per serving: 403 calories, 7.2g fat, 74g carbs, 13.9g fiber, 9.1g sugars, 17.6g protein,

Slimming: Omit coconut milk and use 14-oz (1¾-cups) soy or almond milk plus a drop or two of coconut extract (optional).
Make Ahead: Yes.

Portobello Polenta Bowls

Serves 4

Total Time: 15 min Active Time: 7 min

PREP:

  • 4-6 garlic cloves
  • 8 oz brown mushrooms
  • 1 can (15-oz) white beans
  • 1 cup dry polenta
  • 1/2 cup soy or almond milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 8 oz fresh spinach
  • garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 jar (24-oz) marinara sauce
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (or vegan parmesan)
  • mince
  • slice thick
  • drain, rinse
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  • warm
  •  

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Make Polenta: Bring 2 cups water to a boil. Reduce to low. Slowly whisk in 1 cup polenta. Keep stirring to break down clumps. Add milk (about ½-cup) to thin out. Add salt to taste. Set aside.
  2. Line a skillet with ¼-cup broth.
  3. Sauté garlic for 1 minute or so.
  4. Add mushrooms and thyme, and cook until mushrooms are soft and release their juices, adding broth as needed.
  5. Add spinach and use tongs to stir and incorporate until it cooks down.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, plus garlic powder if you want a strong garlic flavor.
  7. Toss in beans OR leave separate.
  8. Divide polenta equally among 4 bowls and top with warm marinara sauce.
  9. Add spinach mixture and beans.
  10. Sprinkle with nutritional yeast (or vegan parmesan) and serve.

Per serving: 427 calories, 7.6g fat, 70.5g carbs, 13.7g fiber, 16.7g sugars, 19.4g protein,

Notes: Use dry polenta, not pre-made, for this recipe. If you can't find dry polenta, you can use corn grits.
Make Ahead: Spinach-mushroom mixture. For best results, make polenta to order. If making polenta ahead, add water or milk when reheating. Store ingredients separately.

Street Corn Pasta

Serves 4

Total Time: 15 min Active Time: 5 min

PREP:

  • 1 bunch green onions
  • 8 oz dry pasta
  • 1 1/2 cup corn
  • slice
  •  
  •  

    Queso Blanco - Family

  • 2 tbsp COLD water
  • 1 cup soy or almond milk
  • 1 can (4-oz) diced green chiles
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1-2 tsp miso paste
  • 1/2 tsp chipotle powder
  • 1/2 lemon

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Cook pasta as directed (see package), adding corn 1 minute before pasta is done.
  2. Drain, rinse, and return to pot.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare Queso Blanco: In a saucepan, whisk together all ingredients. Bring almost to a boil, stirring regularly, then turn off heat.
  4. Pour prepared Queso Blanco over cooked pasta and corn, stirring well.
  5. Divide equally among 4 bowls.
  6. Garnish with green onions and serve.

Per serving: 381 calories, 4.6g fat, 69.1g carbs, 13g fiber, 5.8g sugars, 20.6g protein,

Slimming: Use cauliflower rice instead of pasta (optional). Cook cauliflower rice until just soft. Pour Queso Blanco ingredients over cauliflower rice and stir together. Cook until creamy, then add corn.
Make Ahead: Yes. Best served warm.

Vegan Palak Paneer

Serves 4

Total Time: 25 min Active Time: 10 min

PREP:

  • 2 cup cooked rice
  • 1 onion
  • 1 tomato
  • 3-4 garlic cloves
  • 1-inch fresh ginger root
  • 10 oz frozen spinach
  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 1-2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp sambal oelek (or red pepper flakes)
  • 1/2 cup lite coconut milk
  • 1 cup peas
  • 1/4 cup cashews

    Tofu Paneer

  • 15 oz extra-firm tofu
  • 1 lemon
  • 2-3 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 tsp miso paste (optional)

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Cook rice if not using precooked.
  2. Prepare Tofu Paneer: Wrap tofu in a kitchen cloth and place under a heavy pot or cutting board for 20 minutes. Unwrap and cube. Toss cubes with juice for HALF the lemon (about 2 tbsp), nutritional yeast, and miso. Marinate overnight if possible.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F and line baking sheet with parchment paper.
  4. Place tofu cubes on baking sheet and bake 20-40 minutes, or until golden and crispy, turning a few times to cook all sides.
  5. Microwave spinach, taking care to press out any excess water. (If you like your palak paneer silky smooth, blend cooked spinach with broth as needed to make a thick paste).
  6. Line a skillet with ¼-cup water.
  7. Sauté onions, tomatoes, garlic, and ginger, until onions are translucent.
  8. Stir in ketchup and spices, plus sambal oelek (or a pinch of red pepper flakes).
  9. Add cooked spinach, coconut milk, and peas.
  10. Cook uncovered until it’s thick and creamy, 3-10 minutes.
  11. Add salt and pepper to taste, plus more sambal oelek if desired.
  12. Fold in Tofu Paneer before serving and serve over cooked rice (½-cup per serving).
  13. Garnish with cashews and squeeze lemon juice (1-2 slices) over top before eating (optional).

Per serving: 393 calories, 13.6g fat, 48.9g carbs, 7.7g fiber, 6.3g sugars, 24.7g protein,

Notes: For Soy-Free, omit Tofu Paneer and add 1 can (15-oz) chickpeas (drain, rinse) in Step 9.
Slimming: Use soy or almond milk instead of coconut milk. Omit cashews and increase rice to ¾-cup per serving (add another 1 cup cooked rice (total-shared)).
Make Ahead: Yes. Store rice and Tofu Paneer separately.

Jerk'd Sweet Potatoes

Total Time: 15 min Active Time: 5 min

PREP:

  • 4 cooked sweet potatoes
  • 12 oz frozen mango (or 2 fresh)
  • 2 can (15-oz each) chickpeas
  • 1 bunch green onions

    Jerk Marinade - Family

  • 3-4 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • red pepper flakes (optional)

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Microwave sweet potatoes if not precooked.
  2. Prepare Jerk Marinade: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together all ingredients until well-combined.
  3. In another large mixing bowl, mash chickpeas with a fork until coarse and no whole beans are left.
  4. Add ¾ (or less) of the Jerk Marinade.
  5. Stir until well-combined. Set aside.
  6. Slice cooked sweet potatoes down the middle and lay open-faced.
  7. Top with chickpea mixture, green onions, and mango (or eat mango on the side) plus a drizzle of leftover marinade.

Per serving: 400 calories, 2.1g fat, 79.8g carbs, 14.3g fiber, 26.4g sugars, 14.7g protein,

    Orange Sauce - Family

  • 3-4 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2-3 tbsp orange marmalade
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • Sriracha (optional)

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Cook rice if not precooked.
  2. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Wrap tofu in a kitchen cloth and place under a heavy pot or cutting board for 20 minutes. Unwrap and cube.
  4. Spread cubes on parchment paper with space between each cube.
  5. Bake 20-40 minutes, or until golden, turning as needed to cook all sides.
  6. Prepare Orange Sauce: Whisk togetherall ingredients with ⅔-cup water. Taste, adding more soy sauce, marmalade, ginger, garlic, or Sriracha if desired.
  7. Toss steamed broccoli and baked tofu with Orange Sauce and serve over cooked rice.
  8. Top with green onions and cashews.

Per serving: 415 calories, 11.9g fat, 63.2g carbs, 5.2g fiber, 9.9g sugars, 19.7g protein,

Slimming: Substitute 5-oz edamame for cashews.
Make Ahead: Yes. Store sauce separately.

PREP:

  • 1/2 cup cooked lentils
  • 2 cooked sweet potatoes
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 zucchini
  • 1 bag (12-oz) cauliflower florets
  • 3 oz fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup lite coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild curry powder
  • 1/8 tsp ground cinnamon
  •  
  •  
  • dice
  • seed, dice
  • dice
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  
  •  

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Cook lentils if not precooked.
  2. Microwave sweet potatoes if not precooked.
  3. Once cooked, scoop out flesh, dice, or mash into a bowl and set aside.
  4. Steam cauliflower or leave raw.
  5. Line a large skillet with ¼-cup water.
  6. Sauté carrots, peppers, and zucchini to desired tenderness.
